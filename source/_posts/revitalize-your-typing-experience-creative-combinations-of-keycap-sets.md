 Tired of looking at the same old keyboard every day? That may seem like an odd question, but think about it. Many of us use our keyboards all day, every day, for work, school, and gaming. It's good to switch up the look of your workstation every once in a while, but buying a whole new keyboard can be overkill—and expensive if you're using [mechanical keyboards](https://instagram-clips.techidaily.com/social-sensation-best-insta-stories-effects/).

 One relatively cheap way to change things up is to swap out your keycaps! Maybe you're still using the same set you got with your keyboard, or you have one set you've been using for a while. Better yet, you don't need to swap all the keys out. Why not mix and match instead?

 Mixing and matching keycaps is always a fun experience. You can go through your sets, see which ones complement each other, and create a keyboard that's uniquely yours. Sometimes even something as simple as swapping out the Escape and Enter keys with [some accent colors](https://cannonkeys.com/collections/cannonkeys-keycaps/products/nicepbt-accent-kits) can change the entire look of your board. And if you're like me, you have a bunch of sets just sitting in boxes anyway. Change it up!
